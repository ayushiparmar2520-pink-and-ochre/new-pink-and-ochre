It Ends With Us by Colleen Hoover — A Book Review That Will Stay With You

By Aayushi Parmar · June 2026 · 8 min read

Home>Books>It Ends With Us Book Review
⚠️ Content Warning

This book and this review discuss themes of domestic abuse and emotional manipulation. If these are sensitive topics for you, please read with care. If you or someone you know needs support, please reach out to the National Domestic Violence Hotline: 1-800-799-7233.

📖 TitleIt Ends With Us
✍️ AuthorColleen Hoover
📅 PublishedAugust 2, 2016
🏷️ GenreContemporary Romance / Women's Fiction
📄 Pages384 pages
⭐ Aayushi's Rating★★★★★ (5/5)
💬 One Line"The book that will break your heart and then teach you how to put it back together."

"Some books you finish and immediately want to press into the hands of every woman you love. It Ends With Us is that book."

I picked it up knowing very little — just that people cried, that BookTok could not stop talking about it, and that Colleen Hoover had apparently broken a million hearts and somehow made them feel grateful for it. What I did not expect was how quietly it would get under my skin. How it would make me reconsider things I thought I already understood about love, about strength, and about the complicated, heartbreaking reality of staying when everything in you knows you should go.

This is my honest review — spoilers clearly marked, no judgement, all feelings.

Quick Take

It Ends With Us is a beautifully written, emotionally devastating story about a young woman who finds herself in an abusive relationship and must find the courage to break the cycle. It is not a comfortable read — it is not supposed to be. But it is one of the most important, compassionate, and human books about love and abuse ever written for a mainstream audience. Read it.

Key Takeaways
  • A story about love, strength, and breaking generational cycles of abuse
  • Colleen Hoover based the story on her own mother's real experiences
  • Writing is emotionally raw, fast-paced, and deeply human
  • The ending is not a happy ending — it is the right ending
  • Difficult themes handled with exceptional sensitivity and compassion
  • A book that changes how you think about love, not just how you feel about it

What Is It Ends With Us About?

Lily Bloom has just moved to Boston to start fresh — she has opened a flower shop, left her hometown behind, and is quietly building the life she always wanted. Then she meets Ryle Kincaid on a rooftop at a party. He is a neurosurgeon — charming, direct, and completely clear that he does not do relationships. And yet.

As Lily falls for Ryle, she also begins writing about her first love — Atlas Corrigan — in a series of diary entries addressed to Ellen DeGeneres. These flashbacks weave through the present-day story, slowly revealing who Lily is, where she came from, and why the choices she makes in her relationship with Ryle matter so much more than they first appear.

It Ends With Us is about the cycle of abuse — how it begins, why it continues, and what it costs to break it. It is told with such tenderness and such clarity that it becomes impossible to judge Lily, or Ryle, or any of the women who came before her. That is the extraordinary thing about this book. Hoover does not ask you to hate anyone. She asks you to understand.

✦

The Writing — What Colleen Hoover Does Best

Colleen Hoover's writing is deceptively simple. She does not reach for difficult words or complex sentences. She writes the way you think — directly, emotionally, with humour and heartbreak often sitting in the same paragraph. This accessibility is not a weakness. It is precisely what makes the book so powerful.

The diary entry structure — Lily writing to Ellen DeGeneres about Atlas — is one of the most clever narrative devices I have encountered in contemporary romance. It creates an intimacy that feels immediate and real, while also allowing Hoover to slowly reveal backstory without ever making it feel like exposition.

"She writes the way you think — directly, emotionally, with humour and heartbreak often sitting in the same paragraph."

The pacing is also exceptional. For a book dealing with heavy subject matter, it moves quickly. You are never stuck. You never put it down. And Hoover knows exactly when to give you a moment of warmth before she breaks your heart again.

✦

The Characters

Lily Bloom

Our Narrator

Lily is one of the most fully realised female protagonists in contemporary fiction. She is not perfect — she makes choices that are hard to watch — but she is completely, painfully human. Her voice is warm and witty and heartbreaking all at once, and her growth across the novel is one of the most quietly devastating things Hoover has ever written.

Ryle Kincaid

The Complication

Ryle is the most difficult character in the book to write about — because Hoover makes him genuinely likeable, which is entirely the point. He is not a monster. He is a person with wounds who makes devastating choices. The discomfort you feel towards him is deliberate, and important.

Atlas Corrigan

The First Love

Atlas appears mostly in Lily's diary entries, and yet he feels entirely present throughout the novel. His relationship with Lily — built in a different time, under impossible circumstances — provides the emotional foundation against which everything in the present story is measured.

✦

The Themes — What This Book Is Really About

On the surface, It Ends With Us looks like a romance. It has all the architecture of one — the meet-cute, the slow burn, the love triangle, the beautiful setting. But what Hoover is really writing about is far more complex and far more important than any romantic plot.

1.The Cycle of Abuse

Hoover based this book on her own mother's experience of abuse, which she revealed in the author's note at the end. The way she portrays the cycle — the escalation, the apology, the forgiveness, the repetition — is clinically accurate and emotionally devastating. She never sensationalises it. She simply shows it.

2.Why People Stay

This is the question the book answers better than almost anything I have read. Not why people are weak, or foolish, or in denial. But why love is complicated, and how the person you love can also be the person who hurts you, and how those two truths can exist together for a very long time before something finally breaks.

3.Breaking Generational Patterns

The title It Ends With Us refers to this — the decision to be the person who breaks a pattern that has been repeating across generations. This is the book's most powerful idea, and the one that stays with you longest after the last page.

✦

What I Loved

♥

The Author's Note

Read the author's note at the end. Do not skip it. Hoover's personal connection to this story transforms how you understand every page. It is one of the most moving things I have read in any book.

♥

The Ending

The ending of this book is not what you want. It is what Lily needs. And the fact that Hoover had the courage to write it — and that so many readers initially resented it and then came to understand it — says everything about what this book achieves.

♥

The Humour

Even in its darkest moments, this book is funny. Lily's voice has a warmth and wit that stops the story from ever becoming unbearable. This balance is incredibly difficult to achieve, and Hoover does it with apparent effortlessness.

♥

The Lily and Atlas Dynamic

Their relationship — built in a different time, through notes left in a journal — is the most tender and quietly heartbreaking love story in the book. Every scene between them earns its place.

♥

That It Exists at All

A mainstream commercial romance novel that handles domestic abuse with this level of nuance, compassion, and accuracy is rare. The fact that it reached millions of readers — including many who had never thought about these issues before — is extraordinary.

✦

What I Found Difficult (Spoiler-Free)

This section is completely spoiler-free. I discuss the reading experience, not specific plot events.

This book is not an easy read. There were moments I had to put it down and take a breath. Not because the writing fails — precisely because it succeeds. Hoover puts you so completely inside Lily's experience that there is no comfortable distance between you and what is happening to her.

Some readers have found the middle section of the book difficult — the period where Lily is making choices that are hard to watch. I found it devastating and essential. But I want to be honest that it is not a comfortable read, and that is entirely the point.

If you are in or have been in an abusive relationship, this book may be very close to the bone. The content warning at the top of this review is genuine. Please read with care.

✦

Should You Read It?

✅ Read It If...

  • You want a book that genuinely makes you think
  • You are interested in emotionally complex contemporary fiction
  • You want to understand the cycle of abuse through the lens of a deeply human story
  • You loved The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo or Normal People

⚠️ Be Mindful If...

  • You are currently in or recently left an abusive relationship
  • Depictions of emotional and physical abuse are triggering for you
  • You need your books to have a traditional happy ending
